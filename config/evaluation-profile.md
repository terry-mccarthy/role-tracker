# Offer Evaluation Profile — Terry McCarthy

_Captured 2026-04-30. This is the profile used to score and compare any senior engineering leadership offers._

## Top-line orientation

- **Primary driver:** Lifestyle & balance
- **Time horizon:** Stepping stone — next role should set me up for VP Eng / CTO within 2–3 years
- **Geography:** Sydney-based or fully remote — no relocation
- **Hybrid stance:** Open to up to 3 days office; 5-day RTO is a hard no

## Compensation

- **TC floor:** AUD $250k–$300k (base + super + cash bonus)
- **Cash-first.** Strong preference for high base over equity; equity is upside, not core comp
- **Sceptical of bonuses** — discount STI/LTI heavily, count only what's contractually guaranteed
- **Benefits that move the needle:** L&D / conference budget, generous leave (annual, parental, sabbatical)

## Role & scope

- **Org size:** Multi-squad — 15–40 engineers (Head of level)
- **Time mix:** People leadership + strategy/roadmap. Not seeking hands-on coding.
- **Domain:** Agnostic — leadership challenge matters more than the technical domain
- **Reporting line:** Report to CTO

## Company fit

- **Stage:** Series B–C scaleup
- **Size:** 50–1,000 employees
- **Industries (in preference order):** Healthcare / climate / impact, AI/ML-native, B2B SaaS
- **Cultural must-haves:** High-trust low-politics, strong engineering craft, wellbeing & sustainability

## Lifestyle constraints

- **Office cadence:** Up to 3 days/week
- **Commute:** 30–45 min one-way max
- **Sustainable hours:** ~40 hrs/week
- **Travel:** Minimal only — 1–2 trips/year max

## Manager, peers, growth

- **Manager (CTO) attributes:** Trust + autonomy, strong technical depth, will act as career coach
- **Exec team weight:** Mostly the CTO matters — can manage around the rest if the CTO is great
- **Peers:** Critical — want strong engineering peers, not to be the most senior by default
- **Growth wanted:** Path to VP / CTO + funded executive coaching

## Hard nos (walk-away regardless of comp)

- Crypto, gambling, adult, or weapons industries
- Hero / burnout culture (always-on expectations, weekend work normalised)
- 5-day in-office mandate
- Founder/CEO red flags (toxic behaviour, bullying, public dramas)

## Risk tolerance

- **Runway:** Moderate — 12–18 months runway with a credible raise plan is fine; no fragile early-stage bets
- **Trust vs ink:** Depends on the person. Title and scope must be contractual; growth trajectory can be verbal if the commitment-maker is credible.

## Scoring weights

| Dimension | Weight |
|---|---|
| Lifestyle (hybrid, hours, commute, travel) | 25% |
| Compensation (base, total cash, benefits) | 20% |
| Scope & growth path (size, level, VP/CTO trajectory) | 20% |
| People (manager fit, peer strength) | 20% |
| Company & mission (stage, industry, culture, runway) | 15% |

## Tensions to surface in any offer review

- **Lifestyle-first vs stepping-stone ambition.** Top driver is lifestyle (~40 hr weeks, 3 office days), but the goal is a 2–3 year springboard to VP/CTO. Roles that meaningfully accelerate the trajectory often demand more hours/travel.
- **Cash-first vs scaleup stage.** Series B–C scaleups typically lean equity-heavy. If a scaleup offer underweights cash, flag it.
- **Multi-squad scope vs CTO reporting line.** Multi-squad (Head of) under a CTO often means 1–2 layers of EMs reporting in. Verify the org chart actually supports that scope rather than a thin "Head of" title over one squad.
